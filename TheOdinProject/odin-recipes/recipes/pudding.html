Purin (Japanese Custard Pudding)

Description

Topped with a deliciously bittersweet caramel sauce, Purin is Japan’s take on crème caramel. This silky smooth custard is a delightful homemade dessert that's sure to impress!

Ingredients

For the Caramel Sauce

  • ▢70 g sugar (about 5 Tbsp; use only granulated sugar)
  • ▢1 Tbsp water (at room temperature)
  • ▢1 Tbsp hot water

For the Custard

  • ▢400 ml whole milk (1⅔ cups)
  • ▢100 ml heavy cream (6 Tbsp plus 2 tsp)
  • ▢½ tsp pure vanilla extract
  • ▢3 large eggs
  • ▢70 g sugar (about 5 Tbsp)

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 300ºF (150ºC). Boil 3–4 cups of hot water for the bain-marie.
  2. In a small saucepan, combine sugar and water for the caramel sauce. Heat on medium low until dissolved, then increase heat and swirl until it turns an amber color.
  3. Carefully add hot water to the caramel, then pour into molds to create a thin layer.
  4. For the custard, heat milk, cream, and vanilla until simmering. In a bowl, whisk eggs and sugar together.
  5. Slowly add the hot milk mixture to the egg mixture while whisking continuously, then strain to remove lumps.
  6. Evenly pour the custard into the molds over the caramel.
  7. Place the molds in a baking dish and fill with hot water halfway up the sides. Bake for 35-45 minutes until set.
  8. Cool completely on a wire rack, then refrigerate for 2-3 hours before serving.
